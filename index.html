Ramadan

  • Islam is built upon five: the testimony that none has the right to be worshipped but Allah and that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah, establishing prayer, giving zakah, performing Hajj, and fasting Ramadan.
    Sahih al-Bukhari, Hadith 8
    Sahih Muslim, Hadith 16
  • Whoever fasts Ramadan out of faith and in the hope of reward, his previous sins will be forgiven.
    Sahih al-Bukhari, Hadith 38
    Sahih Muslim, Hadith 760

Six Days of Shawwal

  • Whoever fasts Ramadan and then follows it with six days of Shawwal, it is as if he fasted for a lifetime.
    Sahih Muslim, Hadith 1164
  • Whoever fasts six days after Eid al-Fitr will have completed the year: whoever does a good deed will have ten times the like thereof.
    Sunan Ibn Majah, Hadith 1715
  • Whoever fasts the month of Ramadan and then follows it with six days of fasting in the month of Shawwal, it will be as if he has fasted for the entire year.
    Sahih Muslim, Hadith 1164

Days of Arafah and Ashura

  • Abu Qatada reported the Messenger (ﷺ) said: I seek from Allah that fasting on the day of 'Arafa may atone for the sins of the preceding and the coming years, and I seek from Allah that fasting on the day of Ashura may atone for the sins of the preceding year.
    Sahih Muslim, Hadith 1162

White Days

  • The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ advised Abu Dharr (RA): If you fast any part of the month, then fast the thirteenth, fourteenth and fifteenth.
    Jami` at-Tirmidhi, Hadith 761
  • The Messenger of Allah said Abu Dharr: Fasting three days of every month is like fasting for a lifetime.
    Sahih al-Bukhari, Hadith 1975
    Sahih Muslim, Hadith 1162
    Sunan an-Nasa'i, Hadith 2420
    Sunan Ibn Majah, Hadith 1708
  • Abu Hurairah (RA) said: My close friend (the Prophet ﷺ) advised me to do three things: to fast three days of every month, pray two rak‘ahs of Duha, and pray Witr before sleeping.
    Sahih al-Bukhari, Hadith 722
    Sunan Abi Dawud, Hadith 1433

Monday and Thursday

  • This specific statement is narrated by Abu Hurairah: Deeds are presented on Mondays and Thursdays, and I love that my deeds be presented while I am fasting.
    Jami` at-Tirmidhi, Hadith 747
    Sunan an-Nasa'i, Hadith 2358
    Riyad as-Salihin, Book 9, Hadith 1256
  • The Prophet was asked about fasting on Monday, and he said: That is the day on which I was born and the day on which revelation came to me.
    Sahih Muslim, Hadith 1162
  • Aishah (RA) reported: The Prophet used to be keen to fast on Mondays and Thursdays.
    Jami` at-Tirmidhi, Hadith 745
